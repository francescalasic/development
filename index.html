Design Competencies Development

My Growth as a Designer

Here I describe my development journey across five key competency areas

Business & Entrepreneurship

During my journey, I realized that industrial design must respond to market needs, creating solutions that meet consumer expectations...

I’ve learned how "Business and Entrepreneurship" and "User and Society" are strongly interconnected. Presenting my work was a way to focus on the challenge of capturing users' attention, helping me understand how commercial presentation is key to a product’s success.

In the future, I would like to attend economics and marketing courses to expand my knowledge in this area.

Creativity & Aesthetics

My journey helped me understand how to balance creativity and functionality in design...

A good project must not only be aesthetically pleasing but must also respond to user needs. The visual aspect should be closely tied to practicality and function. Good design improves user experience.

For Project 1, I designed a children’s game manual using a minimalist graphic style and stylized characters to keep attention on the described actions. Different colors were used in each scene for better visibility and contrast.

Math, Data & Computing

I learned that creativity and innovation must be supported by math and physics to guide design decisions...

For example, when designing sustainable packaging, I studied the physical properties of materials. Geometry was key when designing the game box for Project 1 to ensure functionality and aesthetic appeal. Rectangles with rounded edges were used to secure items safely.

In designing the wearable prototype, I applied physics to ensure it was ergonomic and safe for children in motion.

Technology & Realization

Technological skills are essential to turning ideas into real solutions...

I used 3D modeling and digital prototyping to develop and refine my designs. For Project 1’s wearable device, I iterated on fit and function, improving usability with a shoelace strap and screen housing. Arduino helped prototype color sensors and communication components.

User & Society

User-centered design has been central to my journey...

From the Human-Centered Design course, I learned to conduct user interviews and analyze their needs. For Project 1, I applied these skills to understand user expectations. This experience taught me to plan questions carefully and highlighted how involving users improves both process and outcome.
